Dr. Kaundal's research: information to inference in OMICS data science

Biological data are accumulating faster than people’s capacity to analyze them. Our research interests and goals revolve around mitigating these bigdata issues in the context of “information to inference” scope. At USU, Dr. Kaundal has developed an independent and collaborative research program in bioinformatics, primarily focusing on computational mining of large multi-dimensional -omics datasets, and computational modeling using supervised (Machine Learning) and unsupervised (Bayesian-based) learning. Our group is actively developing novel tools and software to apply the gained knowledge towards organismal improvement.   • Develop novel computational algorithms to explore cross-kingdom threats and identify novel therapeutic candidates,
  • Investigate the diversity of enzymes in soil metagenomes and develop advanced bioinformatics solutions to predict and recover novel enzyme encoding genes involved with the nitrogen (N) cycle from soil metagenomes,

KAABiL group is one of Utah’s
